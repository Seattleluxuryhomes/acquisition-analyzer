# Why Bidtranslator saves you time and energy

The whole bidding-and-getting-paid process — usually a string of after-hours
chores — collapses into *talk on site → send → signed → paid → booked*. Here is
every place it saves a contractor real hours.

---

## 1. Talk instead of type
**Old way:** Scribble notes on site, drive home, retype the whole bid at the kitchen table.
**Bidtranslator:** Describe the job out loud on your phone — the AI writes and structures it for you.
**Saves:** A night of typing per bid. You never write a proposal from scratch again.

## 2. One bid, two languages
**Old way:** Capture in Spanish, then translate to English by hand (or with a bilingual friend) so the client can read it.
**Bidtranslator:** Talk in your language; the client reads the proposal in theirs — automatically.
**Saves:** The translation step entirely, and the awkwardness of a bid the client can't fully read.

## 3. Priced the moment you finish talking
**Old way:** Add up materials and labor, then do markup math to land on a number.
**Bidtranslator:** Your margin is built in, so a real, priced bid appears instantly — no calculator.
**Saves:** The pricing math on every job, and the mistakes that come with doing it tired.

## 4. Professional proposal, auto-built
**Old way:** Format the bid so it doesn't look like a napkin — logo, layout, clean numbers.
**Bidtranslator:** A polished proposal with your logo, organized scope, and totals is generated for you.
**Saves:** The "make it look professional" hour — and it looks like it came from a seasoned GC.

## 5. Terms & protections already on every bid
**Old way:** Forget to spell out warranty, change orders, allowances, or permits — then eat the cost when a dispute comes up.
**Bidtranslator:** Warranty, change-order policy, allowances, unforeseen-conditions, and payment terms are on every bid by default (editable once in Settings).
**Saves:** Hours of disputes and "but I thought that was included" — set once, protected on every job.

## 6. Signed on the spot
**Old way:** Email a PDF, then chase the homeowner for days to print, sign, scan, and send it back.
**Bidtranslator:** The client reviews and signs on their phone; everyone gets a signed PDF instantly.
**Saves:** Days of follow-up per job — and you close while they're still excited about the project.

## 7. Get paid by card — immediately
**Old way:** Send a separate invoice, wait for a check, drive across town to pick it up, or nudge them again and again for the deposit.
**Bidtranslator:** The client pays the deposit by card the moment they sign — money goes straight to your account.
**Saves:** Hours of invoicing and deposit-chasing, plus the cash-flow gap. You start the job already paid.

## 8. Your books update themselves
**Old way:** Re-type every signed job into QuickBooks by hand — double data entry, or paying a bookkeeper to do it.
**Bidtranslator:** Connect QuickBooks once; signed bids flow in automatically as Estimates.
**Saves:** Hours of bookkeeping each week and the errors of manual re-entry — your books stay current on their own.

## 9. Everything in one place, always saved
**Old way:** Juggle a notebook, phone photos, text threads, a spreadsheet, and a signed-paper pile.
**Bidtranslator:** Notes, photos, bids, signatures, and payments all live in one app that remembers everything across your devices.
**Saves:** The hunt for "where did I put that bid" — nothing gets lost.

## 10. It all happens on your phone, on site
**Old way:** The real work of bidding happens back at the office, after hours.
**Bidtranslator:** Capture, build, price, send, sign, and collect — all from your phone, before you leave the driveway.
**Saves:** The trip back to the desk. You get your evenings back.

---

### The one-line version
**Talk through the job, and Bidtranslator hands you a finished, priced, professional bid — in your client's language. They sign and pay on their phone, and it lands in your books automatically. You bid more jobs in less time, and get your nights back.**
